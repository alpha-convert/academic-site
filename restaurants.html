Joe's Restuarant List
This is a list of some of the better restaurants I've had the pleasure to visit while living in Philly. It's heavily inspired by (and overlaps significantly with) Penn CS Prof Lyle Ungar's legendary list of Philly restaurants, which is required reading for any food-loving new arrival to the city.

Old City

  • Amada: Modern spins on classic Spanish tapas & other plates. Don't miss the lamb meatballs.
    [2nd and Chestnut, $$$]
  • Fork: Refined New American food that never disappoints. Owned and operated by the legendary Ellen Yin, one of Philly's many great restauranteurs (and Penn alumnus!).
    [3rd and Market, $$$]
  • Forsythia: Modern French. Truly inventive and fun menu backed up by fantastic service. Opened in early 2020 so still figuring some things out.
    [2nd and Chestnut, $$$]

Rittenhouse & Fittler Square

  • Parc: A massive french bistro good for all occasions. If you like Balthazar in NYC or Le Diplomate in DC, you'll feel right at home at Parc.
    [18th and Locust, $$$]
  • Rouge: Another option on Rittenhouse. The burger is famous, but I'd pick Parc over Rouge any day of the week.
    [18th and Locust, $$$]
  • a.kitchen: Another restaurant in the Ellen Yin empire. Great cocktails available in the attached bar.
    [18th and Walnut, $$$]
  • The Dandelion: Previously thought to be impossible, The Dandelion proves that British food can be made to taste good.
    [18th and Sansom , $$]
  • Ambrosia: Adorable Italian BYOB tucked between two colonial-era buildings on the river. Handmade pasta, the works. Not to be missed.
    [18th and Locust, $$]
  • Rosy's Taco Bar: Always packed with yuppies, MBA students, and undergrads, but don't let that fool you. The tacos here are for real, as confirmed by multiple Californians.
    [22nd and Walnut, $]
  • Village Whiskey: High-quality pub food, prepared with care. Extensive whiskey selection, as the name suggests.
    [20th and Sansom, $$]
  • Pub and Kitchen: Gastropub with an inventive set of appetizers and small plates.
    [20th and Lombard, $$]

Center City

  • Sampan: Very hip modern pan-asian restaurant. Extensive vegetarian and vegan options.
    [13th and Sansom, $$$]
  • Abe Fisher
  • Bud and Marilyns: Southern comfort food with a retro vibe. Not to be mistaken for the famous Philly dive bar Bob and Barbara's.
    [12th and Locust, $$]

West Philly

  • Renata's Kitchen: Modern takes on Mediterranean & Middle-Eastern. Doubles as a fantastic happy hour spot, in a location just out of the way enough that it's not packed with undergrads.
    [40th and Baltimore, $$]
  • White Dog Caf√©: A Penn staple. The decor is dog-themed, but the classic, farm-to-table american cuisine is much better than dog food.
    [34th and Sansom, $$]

Drinks

  • 1 Tippling Place: Cocktail bar with a nondescript entrance. Inside, you'll find refined cocktails in a parlor setting: big leather armchairs, oil paintings, the works.
    [20th and Chestnut, $$$]
  • Local 44: Classic watering hole for West Philadelphians. You'll meet all sorts at Local 44, and they'll all be fascinating.
    [44th and Spruce, $$]
  • Cira Green: Bar in a public green space atop a parking garage on the Schuylkill river! The drinks are pricey and the food is bad, but you can't beat the views.
    [30th and Chestnut, $]
